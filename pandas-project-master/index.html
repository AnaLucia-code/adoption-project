Pet Adoptions Are Up Due To Coronavirus - What Can Google Trends Tell Us?

Source: Benjamin White/KC Pet Project/Flickr

By Ana Lucia Murillo
Published April 16, 2020 8:00 PM

Anecdotal evidence shows that more Americans are fostering and adopting pets since the coronavirus pandemic began to spread. According to Google Trends data, searches for “pet adoption,” and similar search terms have risen worldwide from a month ago.

But what about across different states in the US? Comparing Google searches for “coronavirus” with searches for “pet,” over the last three months shows that some states with higher numbers of confirmed coronavirus cases have lower rates of searching words like “pet.” The District of Columbia, for example, has a search rate of only 2 (out of 100), a proprietary Google Trends measurement system, and has over 2,00 confirmed cases of coronavirus.

New York state also has one of the lowest rates of searches for “pet,” with a Google Trends value of 3. Yet the state is currently the epicenter of the coronavirus pandemic in the US.

On the other end of the spectrum, South Dakota has a higher value of searches for “pet.” Its value is 5, and the state currently has less than 1,000 confirmed coronavirus cases.

There is obviously a huge gap between searches for the term coronavirus and searches for the term pet. Although anecdotally we know that animal fostering has gone way up in New York City, for example, that’s obscured by how many searches there are for coronavirus in the state.

A challenge of using Google Trends analysis is that you can only view searches as a part of all searches in that area. This means when searches rise for another term, the value for the term we are trying to observe decreases as a result.

An analysis that may show us more going forward is comparing the change in searches for “coronavirus” and searches for “pet” over a period of time.
